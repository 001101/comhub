              Julian Harrer 29.04.2016 - 16:05

              I think you have to talk with your provider. Here are the suggestions from the githug ticket:

              - Get your hosting provider to install the PHP mcrypt extension. This reads internally from /dev/urandom but not in a way that the open_basedir setting inhibits. I just tested with PHP 5.4 and restrictive open_basedir and mcrypt_create_iv() appeared to work.
              On this Ubuntu system it would be (for a sysop as simple as) apt-get install php5-mcrypt and restart FPM

              - Get your hosting provider to append ":/dev/urandom" to the open_basedir INI setting and restart FPM
